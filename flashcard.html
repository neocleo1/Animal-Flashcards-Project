Red Panda

Red pandas are skilled climbers, using trees for shelter, to escape predators and to sunbathe.

Scientific Name: Ailurus fulgens
Average Length: 31 to 44 in (with tail)
Average Lifespan: 8 to 10 years
Habitat: High-altitude, temperate forests in the high mountains

Small, arboreal mammals with striking reddish-brown fur, black bellies and limbs, and white markings on their faces. They have a long, bushy tail with rings of red, brown, and white. Red pandas are known for their acrobatic skills and are primarily found in the Eastern Himalayas and southwestern China.

River Otters

Otters can swim up to seven miles per hour and dive down 60 feet.

Scientific Name: Lontra canadensis
Average Length: 40 to 60 inches (with tail)
Average Lifespan: 8 to 12 years
Habitat: Areas with permanent freshwater sources like rivers, lakes, ponds, streams, marshes, and wetlands

River otters are semi-aquatic mammals known for their playful nature and strong swimming abilities. They are adapted to both land and water, with streamlined bodies, webbed feet, and a flattened, muscular tail. They often live in burrows along riverbanks.

Red Lionfish

The 13 long dorsal fins along the spine of the lionfish are venomous and perfect deterrent for predators.

Scientific Name: Pterois volitans
Average Length: 12 to 15 inches
Average Lifespan: 10 years
Habitat: Indo-Pacific region, specifically near coral reefs, rocky areas, and other hard structures

Lionfish are reef fish known for their striking appearance and venomous spines. They have long, flowing fins and distinctive zebra-like stripes of red, brown, and white. These fish are native to the Indo-Pacific and Red Sea, but have become invasive species in the Atlantic and Caribbean, posing a threat to native ecosystems.

Pied-billed Grebe

Pied-billed grebes are are not strong fliers and prefer to escape predators by diving.

Scientific Name: Podilymbus podiceps
Average Length: 12 to 15 inches | Wingspan: 17.7 to 24.4 inches
Average Lifespan: 3 to 5 years
Habitat: Sluggish rivers, freshwater marshes, lakes, and estuaries

The pied-billed grebe is a small, brown waterbird known for its diving skills and ability to camouflage in aquatic vegetation. They are solitary birds, often found near marshy areas and have a distinct call resembling "cuck-cuck-cuck, cow-cow-cow". They build floating nests of vegetation and raise their young, often carrying them on their backs or tail.
